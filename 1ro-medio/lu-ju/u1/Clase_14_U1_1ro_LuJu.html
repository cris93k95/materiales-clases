Unidad 1 — The World of Technical Work

📘 OA1 · OA9 · OA7 ⏱ 90 min 🧭 Guía de estudiante completa

🎯 Objetivo de la clase

Comparar información de textos sobre profesionales técnicos de diferentes países, identificando similitudes y diferencias culturales.

🟢 Inicio (15 min)
💬 Discussion: "Is technical work the same everywhere in the world?"

"Do you think a mechanic in Germany does the same work as a mechanic in Chile?"

🔵 Desarrollo (60 min)

📖 Activity 1 — Jigsaw Reading: International Professionals (45 min)

📖 How Jigsaw Reading Works:

1. Each group reads a different text about a professional from a different country.

2. "Expert groups" discuss and prepare a summary.

3. "Mixed groups" share information and complete the table together.

Text 1: Hans — Automotive Engineer, Germany

Hans works at a BMW factory in Munich. He has worked there since 2015. He specializes in electric vehicle technology. He uses robotic assembly lines and computerized diagnostics. German engineering is famous for precision. Hans used to work on diesel engines, but now he focuses on electric motors. He says: "The future of automotive is electric."

Text 2: Yuki — Electronics Technician, Japan

Yuki works at a semiconductor factory in Osaka. She has built microchips for 8 years. Japanese factories are very clean and organized — workers wear special suits to avoid dust. Yuki used to assemble circuit boards by hand. Now robots do most of the work, and she programs and monitors them. She says: "Technology changes fast. You must keep learning."

Text 3: Camila — Electrician, Chile

Camila works installing solar panels in the Atacama Desert. She has specialized in renewable energy since 2019. Chile has some of the best solar radiation in the world. Camila used to install traditional wiring. Now she designs and installs complete solar systems. She says: "Solar energy is Chile's future. English helps me read the technical manuals."

Text 4: Mike — Graphic Designer, USA

Mike works at a digital printing company in New York. He has used 3D printing technology for 5 years. American print shops used to focus on paper products. Now many offer 3D printing for prototypes and packaging. Mike says: "Print is not dead — it's evolving. We used to print only on paper. Now we print on plastic, metal, and even food!"

Text 5: Jake — Industrial Mechanic, Australia

Jake works at a mining company in Western Australia. He has maintained heavy machinery for 12 years. Australian mining operations are some of the largest in the world. Jake used to repair machines manually. Now he uses drones to inspect equipment in dangerous areas. He says: "Technology makes mining safer. I have used drones since 2020."
