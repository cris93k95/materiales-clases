🎯 Objetivo de la clase

Comprender y utilizar la estructura 'used to + infinitive' para comparar prácticas técnicas del pasado con las actuales.

🟢 Inicio (15 min)
🖼️ Compare: Old workshop (1950-1970) vs. Modern workshop.

"What has changed?" · "Before, they used hand tools. Now, they use machines."

🔵 Desarrollo (60 min)

📖 Activity 1 — Grammar: Used to + Infinitive (15 min)

Structure: Subject + used to + infinitive

For habits or situations in the past that no longer exist.

✅ Affirmative: "Mechanics used to fix cars without computers."

❌ Negative: "They didn't use to have digital multimeters."

❓ Question: "Did they use to weld by hand?"

Contrast: "Mechanics used to use only hand tools. Now they use diagnostic software."

📖 Activity 2 — Reading: Then and Now (25 min)

Then and Now: How Technology Changed the Workshop

Automotive: Mechanics used to diagnose problems by listening to the engine. Now they use computerized diagnostic scanners. They used to change parts manually. Now some workshops use robotic arms.

Industrial: Workers used to operate lathes manually with hand cranks. Now they program CNC machines with computers. They used to measure parts with simple rulers. Now they use digital calipers and laser measurement tools.

Electricity: Electricians used to test circuits with simple test lights. Now they use digital multimeters. They used to draw electrical plans by hand. Now they use CAD software.

Electronics: Technicians used to build circuits with large, individual components. Now they work with tiny surface-mount components. They used to test signals with analog oscilloscopes. Now they use digital ones.

Graphic Arts: Printers used to set text letter by letter (typesetting). Now everything is digital. They used to use only black ink for newspapers. Now they use full-color CMYK printing for everything.
