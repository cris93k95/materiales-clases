📖 Activity 2 — Reading: How to Build a Simple LED Circuit (25 min)

How to Build a Simple LED Circuit

First, gather your materials: a breadboard, an LED, a 220-ohm resistor, two jumper wires, and a 9V battery with a connector.

Second, place the LED on the breadboard. The LED has two legs: the longer leg is the positive side (anode) and the shorter leg is the negative side (cathode).

Then, connect the resistor to the positive leg of the LED. The resistor protects the LED from too much current. Without it, the LED would burn out.

Next, use a jumper wire to connect the other end of the resistor to the positive terminal of the battery.

After that, use another jumper wire to connect the negative leg of the LED to the negative terminal of the battery.

Finally, connect the battery. If everything is correct, the LED should light up! If it doesn't, check the connections and make sure the LED is not backwards.

Important: If you connect the LED the wrong way (reversed polarity), it will not work. If you forget the resistor, the LED will burn out.

📖 Sequence Connectors

These words help us organize steps in order: first, second, then, next, after that, finally.

They are essential in instructive texts!
