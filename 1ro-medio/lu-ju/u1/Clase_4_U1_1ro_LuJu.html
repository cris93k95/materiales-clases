📖 Activity 2 — Case Study: Welding Safety (25 min)

Welding Safety: A Case Study

Tomás is a new worker at an industrial factory in Antofagasta. He started his job two weeks ago. Today, his supervisor asks him to weld two steel plates together.

Tomás is in a hurry because he wants to finish quickly. He picks up the welding torch but does not put on his welding helmet. He also forgets to wear his leather gloves and safety boots.

As Tomás begins to weld, a spark flies into his eye. He drops the torch and screams in pain. Another spark lands on his shoe and burns through the fabric. His coworker, Pedro, rushes to help him.

Pedro takes Tomás to the first aid station. The nurse examines his eye — fortunately, it is only a minor burn, but Tomás cannot work for three days.

The factory supervisor writes an incident report. He identifies the problems: Tomás did not wear a welding helmet, leather gloves, or safety boots. He was rushing and did not follow the safety checklist before starting.

The lesson is clear: PPE (Personal Protective Equipment) is not optional. Every worker must wear the correct safety gear before starting any task. Safety rules exist to protect you.
