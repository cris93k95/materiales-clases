📖 Activity 2 — Reading: Understanding Basic Electrical Circuits (25 min)

Understanding Basic Electrical Circuits

An electrical circuit is a path that allows electricity to flow. Every circuit needs three basic components: a power source (like a battery), a conductor (like a wire), and a load (like a light bulb).

In a closed circuit, electricity flows from the power source through the conductor to the load and back. The light bulb turns on. In an open circuit, the path is broken — maybe a switch is off or a wire is disconnected. The electricity cannot flow, so the light bulb stays off.

A fuse is a safety device. If too much current flows through the circuit, the fuse breaks the circuit to prevent a fire. A circuit breaker does the same job but can be reset — you just flip it back on.

Voltage is the force that pushes electricity through the circuit (measured in volts). Current is the flow of electricity (measured in amperes). Resistance is how much a material opposes the flow of electricity (measured in ohms).

Good conductors like copper and aluminum have low resistance. Insulators like rubber and plastic have high resistance — that's why wires are covered in plastic insulation.
