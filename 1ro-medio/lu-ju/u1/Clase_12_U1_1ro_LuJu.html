📖 Activity 1 — Reading: The Day Everything Went Wrong (30 min)

The Day Everything Went Wrong at Rivera's Workshop

It was a normal Tuesday morning at Rivera's Industrial Workshop in Valparaíso. Three workers were busy: Miguel was welding a steel frame, Ana was operating the lathe, and Jorge was grinding a metal piece.

The first problem started with Miguel. He was welding near a pile of rags soaked in oil. A spark from the welding torch landed on the rags. They caught fire immediately. Miguel shouted for help. Jorge grabbed the fire extinguisher and put out the fire, but it had already damaged some equipment.

While everyone was focused on the fire, Ana's sleeve got caught in the spinning lathe. She screamed. Fortunately, Jorge hit the emergency stop button quickly. Ana's sleeve was torn, but her arm was not seriously injured. She had a minor bruise.

The supervisor, Don Rivera, arrived and was very upset. He investigated what went wrong. The report showed multiple problems: Miguel was not wearing his welding helmet properly, the oily rags should not have been near the welding area, Ana was wearing loose clothing instead of her fitted work uniform, and the workshop's safety inspection had been delayed by two weeks.

Don Rivera closed the workshop for the rest of the day. He organized a mandatory safety training session for the next morning. He said: "Safety is not just a rule — it's a responsibility. Every worker must follow procedures to protect themselves and their coworkers."
