📖 Activity 2 — Reading: From Digital File to Printed Product (25 min)

From Digital File to Printed Product

The printing process starts with a digital file. A graphic designer creates the layout using software like Adobe InDesign. The design includes text, images, and colors arranged on the page.

First, the designer checks the resolution of all images. They must be at least 300 DPI (dots per inch) for good print quality. The color mode must be CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, and Black), not RGB.

Then, the file is sent to the print shop. A technician makes a color proof — a test print to check that the colors are correct. The client reviews and approves the proof.

Next, the technician prepares the printing press. For offset printing, the image is transferred from a plate to a rubber blanket and then to the paper. The technician loads the correct paper stock (substrate) and adjusts the ink levels for each color (C, M, Y, K).

After that, the press runs. The technician checks the first sheets to make sure there are no smudges, misalignment, or color problems.

Finally, the printed sheets go through finishing: cutting to the correct size, folding, and binding. Some products also receive lamination for extra protection and a glossy finish.

The final product is packaged and delivered to the client. The entire process — from digital file to printed product — can take from a few hours to several days, depending on the complexity of the job.
