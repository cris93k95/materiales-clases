📖 Activity 2 — Reading: A Day in a Print Shop (20 min)

A Day in a Print Shop

My name is Valentina and I work at a print shop in Santiago, Chile. I am a graphic arts technician and I love my job because I work with design, colors, and machines every day.

Every morning, I turn on the computers and check the orders for the day. Clients send us digital files for brochures, posters, business cards, and magazines. My first task is to open each file and check the layout, resolution, and color settings. If something is wrong, I contact the client.

After preparing the files, I make a color proof. This is a test print to check that the colors look correct before printing the full order. The client usually approves the proof before we continue.

Then, I set up the printing press. I load the paper stock, adjust the ink levels, and run the machine. I check the first few copies carefully to make sure the quality is perfect — no smudges, no misalignment.

In the afternoon, I work on finishing tasks. This includes trimming the printed sheets to the correct size using a guillotine cutter, and binding the pages for booklets and magazines.

At the end of the day, I clean the machines and prepare the workspace for tomorrow. English is important for me because the design software I use — like Adobe InDesign and Photoshop — has menus and tutorials in English. I also read articles about new printing techniques in English.
