📖 Activity 2 — Reading: A Day in an Electronics Lab (20 min)

A Day in an Electronics Lab

My name is Felipe and I work as an electronics technician at a telecommunications company in Valparaíso, Chile. My job is to assemble, test, and repair electronic devices.

Every morning, I start by reviewing the work orders. Today, I need to repair a communication module and assemble two new circuit boards for a client.

First, I work on the circuit boards. I read the schematic diagram carefully, identify the components I need — resistors, capacitors, transistors, and LEDs — and place them on the board. Then, I use the soldering iron to attach each component. This requires a steady hand and good concentration.

After that, I test the boards using an oscilloscope and a multimeter to make sure they work correctly. If there is a problem, I check each connection and component until I find the fault.

In the afternoon, I repair the communication module. I open it, inspect the internal circuit board, and find a burnt capacitor. I desolder the damaged component and solder a new one in its place.

At the end of the day, I document all my work in a digital report and organize the components in the storage area. English is essential in my job because all the datasheets, technical manuals, and software interfaces are in English.

5. Why is English "essential" and not just "useful" for Felipe?

6. What skills can you infer Felipe needs for his job?
