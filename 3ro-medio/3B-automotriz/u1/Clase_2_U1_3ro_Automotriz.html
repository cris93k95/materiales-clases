📖 Activity 2 — Reading: A Day in an Automotive Workshop (20 min)

A Day in an Automotive Workshop

My name is Roberto and I work as an automotive technician in a workshop in Talca, Chile. I have been working here for five years.

Every morning, I arrive at 8:00 AM and put on my safety equipment: gloves, goggles, and a work uniform. First, I check the schedule to see which vehicles need service today.

My first task is usually an oil change. I lift the car with a jack, drain the old oil, replace the oil filter, and add new oil. I always use a wrench and a socket set for this job.

After that, I inspect the brakes. I remove the wheels and check the brake pads and discs. If they are worn, I replace them. This is very important for the safety of the driver.

In the afternoon, I work on engine diagnostics. I connect a diagnostic scanner to the car's computer and read the error codes. Sometimes, I need to replace spark plugs or fix the electrical system.

At the end of the day, I clean my tools, organize the workshop, and write a short report about each vehicle I worked on. Technical English helps me read the car manuals and understand the diagnostic software, which is always in English.

I love my job because every day is different. The most important thing is to use the right tool for each task and always follow safety procedures.
