📖 Activity 1 — Case Study: The Mysterious Power Outage (25 min)

The Mysterious Power Outage

An office building in Santiago reports that half of the second floor has lost electricity. The lights and outlets on the right side of the floor are not working, but the left side is fine.

Step 1: The electrician checks the main electrical panel. She finds that one circuit breaker has tripped. She resets it, but it trips again immediately.

Step 2: She uses a multimeter to test the circuit. The reading shows a very low resistance — this means there is a short circuit somewhere in the wiring.

Step 3: She disconnects all the outlets and lights on that circuit, one by one, testing after each disconnection. When she disconnects outlet #7, the circuit breaker stays on.

Step 4: She opens outlet #7 and finds the problem: a wire has come loose and is touching the metal box, creating a short circuit. She reconnects the wire properly and insulates it with electrical tape.

Solution: A loose wire touching the metal junction box caused a short circuit that tripped the breaker. Proper reconnection fixed the issue.
