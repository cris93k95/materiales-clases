📖 Activity 2 — Reading: A Day as an Electrician (20 min)

A Day as an Electrician

My name is Camila and I work as an electrician in Rancagua, Chile. I specialize in residential and commercial electrical installations.

Every morning, I review the work orders for the day. I check my toolbox to make sure I have everything: wire cutters, pliers, a multimeter, electrical tape, and screwdrivers.

My first job today is installing new wiring in a house. I read the electrical plan, measure the distances, and run cables through the conduits. I always turn off the main switch before working to stay safe.

After lunch, I go to a small office building to check their circuits. The owner says some outlets are not working. I use my multimeter to test the voltage and find the problem. A wire is disconnected inside the panel. I reconnect it carefully.

Later, I replace an old electrical panel in another house. This is a bigger job. I install new circuit breakers and label each one clearly so the homeowner knows which breaker controls which room.

At the end of the day, I fill out a work report and update the client on what I did. English is useful for me because many electrical components have specifications written in English, and the safety codes I study often come from international standards.

✅ Activity 3 — Comprehension Questions (20 min)

A) Explicit questions:

2. What does Camila always do before working on wiring?

4. Why does Camila label each circuit breaker?

5. Why is safety so important in Camila's job?

6. What can you infer about Camila as a professional?
