📖 Activity 2 — Reading: A Day in an Industrial Workshop (20 min)

A Day in an Industrial Workshop

My name is Andrés and I work as an industrial mechanic at a manufacturing plant in Antofagasta, Chile. The plant produces metal parts for the mining industry.

Every morning, I arrive at 7:00 AM and check the production schedule. I put on my safety equipment: a helmet, safety goggles, steel-toe boots, and gloves. Safety is the number one priority in our workshop.

My first task today is to operate a lathe. I read the blueprint carefully, set the correct speed and feed rate, and begin cutting a metal shaft to the exact measurements. I use a caliper to check the dimensions after every cut. Precision is essential — even a small mistake can ruin the entire piece.

After lunch, I work on welding two metal plates together. I set up the welding machine, adjust the current and voltage, and carefully weld along the joint. The welding mask protects my eyes from the bright light.

Later, I use the milling machine to create a slot in a gear housing. I mount the piece on the machine table, select the right cutting tool, and program the correct depth and width.

At the end of the day, I measure all the finished pieces with a caliper and a micrometer to make sure they meet the specifications. I write a quality report and clean the machines. English is very important because the machine manuals, CNC programs, and quality standards are written in English, and sometimes we communicate with foreign engineers.
