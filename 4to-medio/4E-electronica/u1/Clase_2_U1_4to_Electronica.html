📖 What is a Case Study?

A case study is a detailed story about a real person or situation used as a learning tool. Today, we will read about Diego, a Chilean electronics technician who went from school to maintaining medical equipment at a hospital.

🔵 Desarrollo (60 min)

📖 Activity 1 — Case Study: Building a Tech Career (25 min)

Building a Tech Career

Diego grew up in Concepción, Chile, and was always curious about how electronic devices work. He would take apart old radios and phones to see what was inside. When it was time to choose a specialty at his technical school, he chose electronics without hesitation.

During his studies, Diego learned to read schematic diagrams, solder components onto circuit boards, and test circuits using multimeters and oscilloscopes. He also discovered a passion for programming when his teacher introduced the class to Arduino microcontrollers. The tutorials were all in English, so Diego started learning English on his own using free online resources.

After graduation, Diego did his internship at a telecommunications company. He worked in the maintenance department, repairing communication equipment, replacing faulty circuit boards, and calibrating signal amplifiers. His supervisor noticed that Diego was quick to learn and good at troubleshooting problems.

Diego's first full-time job was at the same company. He worked there for two years. In his free time, he continued learning about Arduino and Raspberry Pi, completing several personal projects: a home automation system and a weather station. All the documentation and community forums for these platforms were in English.

Diego then saw a job posting at a hospital in Santiago. They needed a biomedical electronics technician to maintain and repair medical equipment such as patient monitors, infusion pumps, and diagnostic machines. The position required English because the equipment manuals and software were from international manufacturers. Diego applied and got the job.

Today, Diego works at the hospital maintaining critical medical equipment. He reads datasheets in English every day, communicates with foreign technical support teams, and recently completed an online certification in biomedical equipment maintenance — entirely in English. He says: "Being self-taught in programming and English changed my career. I went from fixing phones to maintaining machines that save lives."

✅ Activity 3 — Comprehension Questions (15 min)

A) Explicit questions:

1. Where did Diego grow up?

2. What personal projects did Diego build in his free time?

3. What type of equipment does Diego maintain at the hospital?

4. How did Diego first start learning English?

B) Critical analysis questions:

5. Why was being "self-taught" an advantage for Diego?

6. Why did the hospital require English for the position?

7. What would you do differently from Diego?

8. How does Diego's story show the connection between curiosity and career success?
