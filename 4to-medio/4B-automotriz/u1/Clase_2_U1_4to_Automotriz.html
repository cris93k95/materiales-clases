📖 What is a Case Study?

A case study is a detailed story about a real person or situation. It is used as a learning tool to analyze experiences, decisions, and results. Today, we will read a case study about a Chilean automotive technician.

🔴 Desarrollo (60 min)

📖 Activity 1 — Case Study: From Workshop to Dealership (25 min)

From Workshop to Dealership

Carlos grew up in Temuco, Chile, and always loved cars. When he was 15, he entered a technical high school (liceo técnico-profesional) and chose the automotive mechanics specialty. During those years, he learned the basics: how to change oil, inspect brakes, and diagnose simple engine problems. His teachers encouraged him to study English because most car manuals were written in English.

After graduating, Carlos did his internship (práctica profesional) at a small independent workshop. There, he gained hands-on experience: he worked on different car brands, learned to use diagnostic scanners, and improved his customer service skills. The owner of the workshop became his mentor and taught him the importance of being punctual and professional.

Carlos' first real job was at the same workshop. He worked there for three years, specializing in engine repair and electrical systems. During this time, he took an online course in English for Automotive Professionals, which helped him read technical bulletins and understand software updates from car manufacturers.

His big break came when a Toyota dealership in Santiago was looking for experienced technicians. Carlos applied, passed a technical test (which was partly in English), and got the job. At the dealership, he received specialized training from Toyota, including a two-week course in Brazil where all the materials were in English.

Today, Carlos is a senior technician at the dealership. He leads a small team, trains new technicians, and handles the most complex diagnostic cases. He recently attended a training seminar in Germany, where he learned about hybrid and electric vehicle technology. English was essential for communicating with engineers from other countries.

Carlos says: "English opened doors I never imagined. Without it, I would still be working only on basic repairs. Now I can access international training, read the latest technical documentation, and grow in my career."

B) Critical analysis questions:

5. Why was English important for Carlos' career?

6. What role did the workshop owner play in Carlos' development?

7. What would you do differently from Carlos?

8. How does Carlos' story show the importance of continuous learning?
