📖 What is a Case Study?

A case study is a detailed story about a real person or situation used as a learning tool. Today, we will read about María, a Chilean electrician who built her own business.

⚡ Desarrollo (60 min)

📖 Activity 1 — Case Study: Powering a Career (25 min)

Powering a Career

María grew up in Copiapó, in northern Chile. As a teenager, she was fascinated by how electricity works and decided to study at a technical high school in the electricity specialty. During her studies, she learned about residential wiring, circuit design, safety regulations, and how to use tools like multimeters and wire cutters.

After finishing school, María did her apprenticeship with a local electrical company. She worked on residential projects: installing wiring in new houses, connecting outlets and switches, and ensuring everything met the Chilean electrical code (SEC regulations). Her supervisor taught her the importance of double-checking every connection.

María's first paid job was at the same company. She worked there for two years, gaining experience in both residential and commercial installations. During this time, she noticed a growing demand for renewable energy in Chile, especially solar power. She decided to specialize.

María took an online certification course in solar panel installation. The course was taught by an international organization, and all the materials — videos, manuals, and exams — were in English. It was challenging, but she studied hard and passed. She learned how to design solar systems, install panels on rooftops, and connect them to the electrical grid.

With her new specialization, María started getting more clients. After three years of saving money and building a reputation, she opened her own small business: a company that installs and maintains solar energy systems for homes and small businesses in the Atacama region.

Today, María employs three technicians and is planning to expand. She regularly reads international electrical codes and technical articles in English to stay updated on new technology. She says: "Learning English was one of the best decisions I made. It gave me access to knowledge that helped me build something of my own."
