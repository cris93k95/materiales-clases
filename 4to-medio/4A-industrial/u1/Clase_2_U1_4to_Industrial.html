📖 What is a Case Study?

A case study is a detailed story about a real person or situation used as a learning tool. Today, we will read about Andrés, a Chilean industrial mechanic who went from apprentice to production line supervisor.

🟢 Desarrollo (60 min)

📖 Activity 1 — Case Study: From Apprentice to Specialist (25 min)

From Apprentice to Specialist

Andrés grew up in Calama, in the heart of Chile's mining region. His father worked at a copper mine, and Andrés always admired the massive machines used in the mining industry. He decided to study industrial mechanics at a technical high school in Antofagasta.

During his studies, Andrés learned the fundamentals: how to operate a lathe and a milling machine, how to read blueprints, how to weld different types of metal, and how to measure parts with calipers and micrometers. His teachers also taught him about workplace safety and the importance of following procedures.

After graduating, Andrés did his apprenticeship at a mining company in the Atacama region. He worked in the maintenance workshop, where he helped repair conveyor belts, replace bearings on large motors, and maintain hydraulic systems. It was hard work, but he learned quickly. His mentor, a senior mechanic named Don Jorge, taught him that "a good mechanic prevents problems before they happen."

Andrés' first permanent job was at the same mining company. He worked in the preventive maintenance team for three years. During this time, the company introduced CNC (Computer Numerical Control) machines, and Andrés volunteered to learn how to program them. The training manual was in English, and the programming software used English commands. Andrés took evening English classes to keep up.

His CNC skills set him apart from other technicians. He was promoted to CNC specialist and later to production line supervisor. In this role, he coordinates a team of five technicians, plans the production schedule, and ensures quality standards are met. He also communicates with foreign engineers when new equipment is installed.

Today, Andrés is studying for an international welding certification (AWS — American Welding Society). The entire course is in English. He says: "English was not easy for me, but it opened doors. In the mining industry, the best technology comes from other countries, and if you can't read the manuals or talk to the engineers, you fall behind."

✅ Activity 3 — Comprehension Questions (15 min)

A) Explicit questions:

1. Where did Andrés grow up?

2. What did Andrés volunteer to learn at his first job?

3. What is Andrés currently studying for?

4. What advice did Don Jorge, his mentor, give him?

B) Critical analysis questions:

5. Why was English important for Andrés' career advancement?

6. Why did Andrés' CNC skills help him get promoted?

7. What would you do differently from Andrés?

8. How does Andrés' story show the value of mentorship in a career?
